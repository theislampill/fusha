# Drill — root + pattern practice (the word family)

**Goal:** take one **root** and walk its **family** — the verb, the maṣdar, the agent and
patient participles, the plurals — so you stop reading words one at a time and start reading them
as predictable variations on a theme. This is the pay-off of
[`morphology-foundations.md`](morphology-foundations.md): once you see the family, each new
surface is a *known shape of a known root*.

**Rule of the drill:** hold the root constant, change the pattern, predict the meaning — then
**class before gloss**. A maṣdar is a noun even when its root is verbal; a participle names a
person/thing, not an action. The root is the theme; the pattern decides whether you say "to X,"
"the X-ing," "the one who X-es," or "the X-ed thing."

> Each family is anchored to a **Qamus address** (`qamus:v###` / `n###`, per
> [`../../qamus/reports/source-address-model.md`](../../qamus/reports/source-address-model.md))
> — the durable study coordinate for that root. Pattern shapes come from
> [`../../sarf/references/verb-measures-table.md`](../../sarf/references/verb-measures-table.md)
> and [`../../sarf/procedures/masdar-participle.md`](../../sarf/procedures/masdar-participle.md);
> root extraction from
> [`../../sarf/procedures/root-decision.md`](../../sarf/procedures/root-decision.md). All glosses
> are `{src:'qamus', kind:'authored', lang:'en'}`. When a member's pattern or sense is uncertain, write
> **PENDING**.

Composition still comes before root family study: strip visible proclitics and suffix pronouns
without mistaking them for radicals, and do not let a host-only family gloss swallow `بِـ`,
`لِـ`, `كَـ`, `وَ`, `فَـ`, or an object suffix. If a surface may be both a proper name and a
common lexical item, concept metadata is only a review flag; the verse decides.

The address ids below (e.g. `qamus:v012`) are study handles for the root family, in the
class-and-frequency order of [`../README.md`](../README.md); open the matching entry on
qamus.dawah.wiki to read the real senses/usage.

## Items (build the family from the root)

### 1. `ك ت ب` — writing — `qamus:v###`
- verb (Form I): `كَتَبَ` "he wrote" · present `يَكْتُبُ` "he writes"
- maṣdar: `كِتَابَة` "the act of writing" (noun) · also `كِتَاب` "a book"
- agent (ism fāʿil): `كَاتِب` "writer" · patient (ism mafʿūl): `مَكْتُوب` "written"
- place-noun: `مَكْتَب` "a desk / office"
**Read it as a family:** one theme (writing), five roles. Note `كِتَاب` and `مَكْتُوب` are
**nouns** — never gloss them "to write."

### 2. `ع ل م` — knowing — `qamus:v###`
- Form I `عَلِمَ` "he knew" → maṣdar `عِلْم` "knowledge" (noun)
- Form II `عَلَّمَ` "he taught" (shadda on `ل` = Form II, causative) → maṣdar `تَعْلِيم`
  "teaching"
- agent `عَالِم` "a scholar / one who knows" · patient `مَعْلُوم` "known"
- intensive: `عَلِيم` "All-Knowing" (mubālaghah, `فَعِيل`)
**Trap:** `عِلْم` (knowledge) vs. `عَالِم` (knower) vs. `عَلَّمَ` (taught) — same root, three
classes. Read the harakāt + the `مـ`/shadda before glossing.

### 3. `أ م ن` — security / trust / faith — `qamus:v###`
- Form I `أَمِنَ` "he was safe" → maṣdar `أَمْن` "safety / security" (noun)
- Form IV `آمَنَ` "he believed" → maṣdar `إِيمَان` "faith" (noun, `إِفْعَال`)
- agent `مُؤْمِن` "a believer" · noun `أَمَانَة` "a trust"
**Guard:** the hamza is the **first radical** — never drop it. `إِيمَان` (root `أ م ن`) is **not**
`أَيْمَان` "oaths" (root `ي م ن`); `norm()` merges them, the hamza seat keeps them apart. See
[`../../sarf/drills/root-detection.md`](../../sarf/drills/root-detection.md).

### 4. `س ل م` — submission / peace — `qamus:v###`
- Form I `سَلِمَ` "he was safe/whole" → noun `سَلَام` "peace"
- Form II `سَلَّمَ` "he greeted / handed over"
- Form IV `أَسْلَمَ` "he submitted" → maṣdar `إِسْلَام` "submission / Islam" (noun)
- agent `مُسْلِم` "one who submits / Muslim" · fem. `مُسْلِمَة`
**Read it as a family:** Form IV `أَسْلَمَ`/`إِسْلَام` carries the religious sense; `سَلَام`
"peace" and `سَلِمَ` "be safe" share the root's theme of wholeness.

### 5. `ر ح م` — mercy — `qamus:v###`
- Form I `رَحِمَ` "he had mercy" → maṣdar `رَحْمَة` "mercy" (noun, with `ة`)
- intensive `رَحْمَٰن` "the Most Merciful" (`فَعْلَان`) · `رَحِيم` "the Bestower of mercy"
  (`فَعِيل`)
- noun `رَحِم` "womb" (same root, kinship sense)
**Trap:** `رَحْمَٰن` and `رَحِيم` are two **intensive adjectives** of the same root, not the
verb — gloss them as the Names/attributes, not "to be merciful."

### 6. `ق و ل` — saying (hollow root) — `qamus:v###`
- Form I `قَالَ` "he said" (the `و` surfaces as `ا`) · present `يَقُولُ` "he says" (`و`
  returns)
- maṣdar `قَوْل` "a saying / word" (noun)
- agent `قَائِل` "a sayer / speaker"
**Weak-root note:** the middle `و` is the **weak radical**; the alif in `قَالَ` is *it*, not a
stray long vowel. (Procedure:
[`../../sarf/procedures/weak-root.md`](../../sarf/procedures/weak-root.md).)

### 7. `ر ج ل` — man / foot (two senses) — `qamus:n###`
- singular `رَجُل` "a man" → broken plural `رِجَال` "men" (`فِعَال`)
- singular `رِجْل` "a foot/leg" → plural `أَرْجُل` "feet" (`أَفْعَال`)
**Trap:** **two singulars** share the consonants, split by the harakāt (`رَجُل` fatḥa-ḍamma vs.
`رِجْل` kasra-sukūn). Bind each plural to the singular it actually pluralizes — never gloss
`رِجَال` "feet." (See
[`../../sarf/procedures/noun-plural-gender.md`](../../sarf/procedures/noun-plural-gender.md).)

### 8. `ن ص ر` — help / victory — `qamus:v###`
- Form I `نَصَرَ` "he helped" → maṣdar `نَصْر` "help / victory" (noun)
- agent `نَاصِر` "a helper" · plural `أَنْصَار` "helpers / supporters"
- patient `مَنْصُور` "(one) helped / given victory"
**Read it as a family:** `نَصْر` (the act/victory), `نَاصِر`/`أَنْصَار` (the helper[s]),
`مَنْصُور` (the helped one) — three roles, one root.

### 9. `ك ف ر` — covering / disbelief — `qamus:v###`
- Form I `كَفَرَ` "he disbelieved / covered" → maṣdar `كُفْر` "disbelief" (noun)
- agent `كَافِر` "a disbeliever" · broken plural `كُفَّار` (`فُعَّال`) / `كَافِرُونَ` (sound pl.)
**Trap:** `كُفْر` is the **noun** "disbelief"; `كَافِر` is the **agent** "disbeliever." Same
root, different class → different gloss vocabulary. Class first.

### 10. `د خ ل` — entering — `qamus:v###`
- Form I `دَخَلَ` "he entered" · present `يَدْخُلُ` "he enters"
- maṣdar `دُخُول` "entering / entry" (noun)
- Form IV `أَدْخَلَ` "he caused to enter / admitted" (causative) → patient `مُدْخَل` "place/act
  of entering"
**Read it as a family:** Form I (enter) vs. Form IV (cause to enter) — the **form** flips the
sense from doing to *causing*; the root's theme is constant.

## Checklist before you leave root-pattern practice

- [ ] For a given root, can I list its **verb · maṣdar · agent · patient · plural** and predict
      each meaning from the pattern?
- [ ] Do I gloss a **maṣdar** as a *noun* and a **participle** as a *person/thing*, never as the
      bare verb?
- [ ] Do I read the **form** (I vs. II vs. IV) off the shadda / `مـ` / prefix, and let it shift
      the sense (e.g. enter → cause-to-enter)?
- [ ] For a **two-sense root** (`ر ج ل`), do I bind each plural to the right singular by its
      harakāt?
- [ ] For a **hamzated/weak/doubled** root, do I keep the seat / spot the weak radical / not
      confuse a geminate with Form II?
- [ ] Did I handle visible attached pieces before mapping the host to a root family?
- [ ] Did I treat proper-name/common-word collisions as context questions, not concept-map
      answers?
- [ ] When a family member's class or sense is uncertain, do I write **PENDING**?
