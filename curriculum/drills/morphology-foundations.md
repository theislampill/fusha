# Drill — morphology foundations (root + pattern)

**Goal:** internalize the single biggest idea in Arabic word-building — **a word is a root
poured into a pattern (wazn)** — and practice reading both off a surface word. Once you can
say "this is root R-D-X in pattern P," you can predict its sisters and bind it to a Qamus
entry.

**Rule of the drill:** separate the two channels. The **root** (usually three radicals) carries
the *area of meaning*; the **pattern** carries the *grammatical role* (verb vs. noun, agent vs.
maṣdar, singular vs. plural). Never read meaning off the consonants alone — the same root in two
patterns is two different words, and the pattern is in the harakāt and the added letters.

> and maṣdar/participle shapes in

> When root or pattern is uncertain, the answer is **PENDING** — name the doubt, do not guess.

## The measuring stick: ف ع ل

Arabic states every pattern against the dummy root **`ف ع ل`** (f‑ʿ‑l): `ف` = first radical,
`ع` = second, `ل` = third. "Pattern `فَاعِل`" means "first radical + long ā + second radical +
kasra + third radical" — so root `ك ت ب` in `فَاعِل` gives `كَاتِب` "writer." Learn to map any
word onto `فَعَلَ` and you have read its pattern. (Procedure:
[`../../sarf/procedures/root-decision.md`](../../sarf/procedures/root-decision.md).)

## Items (give the root, then the pattern)

### 1. The agent pattern `فَاعِل`
**Word:** `كَاتِب`
**Answer:** root `ك ت ب` (writing) in pattern `فَاعِل` → "writer / one who writes" (ism fāʿil,
the active participle). The long ā after the first radical + kasra on the second is the agent
signature.

### 2. Same root, the act itself
**Word:** `كِتَاب`
**Answer:** root `ك ت ب` in pattern `فِعَال` → "a book." Same three radicals as item 1, a
different pattern → a different word. Root = the theme (writing); pattern = the thing/role.

### 3. The patient pattern `مَفْعُول`
**Word:** `مَكْتُوب`
**Answer:** root `ك ت ب` in `مَفْعُول` → "(something) written" (ism mafʿūl, passive participle).
The `مـ` prefix + long ū before the last radical is the patient signature. (See
[`../../sarf/procedures/masdar-participle.md`](../../sarf/procedures/masdar-participle.md).)

### 4. Read the verb's tense off the pattern
**Word:** `يَكْتُبُ` vs. `كَتَبَ`
**Answer:** root `ك ت ب`. `كَتَبَ` = `فَعَلَ` (Form I **past**, "he wrote"); `يَكْتُبُ` =
`يَفْعُلُ` (Form I **present**, "he writes"). The `يـ` prefix + the vowel pattern mark present
tense — the *pattern*, not the root, carries the tense. (Procedure:
[`../../sarf/procedures/verb-form.md`](../../sarf/procedures/verb-form.md).)

### 5. A doubled-letter pattern means a derived form
**Word:** `عَلَّمَ`
**Answer:** root `ع ل م` (knowledge) in `فَعَّلَ` (**Form II**) → "he taught" (made-to-know,
causative). The **shadda** on the middle radical is the whole signal of Form II — do not read
it as an extra root letter. Form I `عَلِمَ` = "he knew." (Measures:
[`../../sarf/references/verb-measures-table.md`](../../sarf/references/verb-measures-table.md).)

### 6. The `مـ` prefix is *not* a radical
**Word:** `مَسْجِد`
**Answer:** root `س ج د` (prostration) in pattern `مَفْعِل` → "a place of prostration / mosque"
(ism makān, place-noun). The leading `مـ` is the place-noun prefix; the radicals are `س ج د`.
Strip the `مـ` before reading the root — a classic affix-masquerades-as-radical trap (see
[`../../sarf/drills/root-detection.md`](../../sarf/drills/root-detection.md)).

### 7. Tāʾ marbūṭa marks gender, not a radical
**Word:** `مُسْلِمَة`
**Answer:** root `س ل م` (submission/peace) in `مُفْعِل` + `ة` → "a Muslim woman." The `ة` is
the **feminine** ending; the masculine is `مُسْلِم`. The `ة` never counts as a root letter.
(Noun gender: [`../../sarf/procedures/noun-plural-gender.md`](../../sarf/procedures/noun-plural-gender.md).)

### 8. Sound vs. broken plural
**Word:** `مُسْلِمُونَ` vs. `رِجَال`
**Answer:** `مُسْلِمُونَ` = a **sound** masculine plural (singular + `ـُونَ` suffix, root `س ل م`
untouched). `رِجَال` = a **broken** plural (root `ر ج ل` re-poured into pattern `فِعَال`; the
singular `رَجُل` is reshaped, not suffixed) → "men." Recognizing which kind of plural you face
is a Level-3/4 skill. (Procedure:
[`../../sarf/procedures/noun-plural-gender.md`](../../sarf/procedures/noun-plural-gender.md).)

### 9. The maṣdar (verbal noun) is a noun
**Word:** `إِسْلَام`
**Answer:** root `س ل م` in the Form IV maṣdar pattern `إِفْعَال` → "submission / Islam." It is
a **noun** (the *act* of submitting), so gloss it as a noun, never "to submit." Class first,
then meaning. (See
[`../../sarf/procedures/masdar-participle.md`](../../sarf/procedures/masdar-participle.md).)

### 10. Weak radical hides in the pattern
**Word:** `قَالَ`
**Answer:** root `ق و ل` (a **hollow** root — middle radical `و`), pattern Form I past. The `و`
surfaces as a long `ا` in `قَالَ` and returns as `و` in `يَقُولُ` / `قَوْل`. The alif you see is
the *weak middle radical*, not a stray long vowel. Weak roots get their own procedure:
[`../../sarf/procedures/weak-root.md`](../../sarf/procedures/weak-root.md).

### 11. Doubled root (geminate)
**Word:** `رَدَّ`
**Answer:** root `ر د د` (the last two radicals identical), shown contracted with a **shadda**:
`رَدَّ` "he returned (it)." The shadda here is two identical radicals merged, not Form II. Tell
it apart by checking whether the doubled letter is the *middle* radical (Form II) or the *last*
(geminate root). (Procedure:
[`../../sarf/procedures/doubled-root.md`](../../sarf/procedures/doubled-root.md).)

### 12. Hamza as a radical
**Word:** `أَمَرَ`
**Answer:** root `أ م ر` (command) — the **hamza is the first radical**, a full consonant.
`أَمَرَ` "he commanded" (Form I). Do not drop the hamza when you record the root, and never let
`norm()` erase its seat. (Procedure:
[`../../sarf/procedures/hamza-root.md`](../../sarf/procedures/hamza-root.md).)

## Checklist before you leave morphology foundations

- [ ] For a given word, can I say its **root** *and* its **pattern** (mapped onto `فَعَلَ`)?
- [ ] Do I strip affixes (`مـ`, `ال`, `ة`, `ـُونَ`) **before** reading the root?
- [ ] Do I read **tense/voice/role** off the pattern, not the consonants?
- [ ] Can I tell a **shadda = Form II** (middle radical) from a **shadda = geminate** (last
      radical)?
- [ ] When a **weak** or **hamzated** radical hides in the surface, do I route to its
      procedure instead of guessing the root?
- [ ] When root *or* pattern is uncertain, do I write **PENDING** and name which one?
