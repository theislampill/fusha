# Drill — sentence foundations (nominal & verbal)

**Goal:** read a whole Arabic clause and name its **type** — *nominal* (starts with a noun) or
*verbal* (starts with a verb) — then point to its parts. This is the rung where words become
meaning: a learner who can split subject from predicate, and subject from object, reads an āyah
instead of decoding tokens.

**Rule of the drill:** classify the sentence **before** you translate it. The first content word
decides the type, and the type decides what you look for next. Get the frame right and the
pieces fall into place; translate first and you will mis-assign the parts.

## The two sentence types

| type | starts with | core parts | example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **jumlah ismiyyah** (nominal) | a noun (or pronoun) | **mubtadaʾ** (subject) + **khabar** (predicate) | `ٱللَّهُ نُورٌ` "Allah is light" |
| **jumlah fiʿliyyah** (verbal) | a verb | **fiʿl** (verb) + **fāʿil** (doer) + (optional) **mafʿūl** (object) | `كَتَبَ ٱلطَّالِبُ` "the student wrote" |

A nominal sentence has **no verb** between subject and predicate — the "is" is understood. A
verbal sentence is **verb-first**: the doer comes *after* the verb. These two frames cover the
overwhelming majority of clauses you will read.

## Items (name the type, then label the parts)

### 1. Bare nominal sentence (no copula)
**Read:** `ٱلْبَيْتُ كَبِيرٌ`
**Answer:** nominal. `ٱلْبَيْتُ` = mubtadaʾ ("the house," definite, with `الْ`), `كَبِيرٌ` =
khabar ("[is] big," indefinite, tanwīn). No verb — supply "is." The definiteness split
(definite subject, indefinite predicate) is the usual shape.

### 2. Verbal sentence, verb-first
**Read:** `ذَهَبَ ٱلْوَلَدُ`
**Answer:** verbal. `ذَهَبَ` = fiʿl ("went," Form I past, root `ذ ه ب`), `ٱلْوَلَدُ` = fāʿil
("the boy") — the doer follows the verb. English reorders to "the boy went"; Arabic does not.

### 3. Verb + doer + object
**Read:** `كَتَبَ ٱلطَّالِبُ ٱلدَّرْسَ`
**Answer:** verbal. `كَتَبَ` fiʿl, `ٱلطَّالِبُ` fāʿil ("the student," ḍamma = nominative doer),
`ٱلدَّرْسَ` mafʿūl bihi ("the lesson," fatḥa = accusative object). The **case endings** tell doer
(ḍamma) from object (fatḥa) — read them. (See
[`../../nahw/procedures/irab-case-mood.md`](../../nahw/procedures/irab-case-mood.md).)

### 4. Pronoun as subject
**Read:** `هُوَ غَفُورٌ`
**Answer:** nominal. `هُوَ` = mubtadaʾ ("He"), `غَفُورٌ` = khabar ("[is] forgiving"). A
detached pronoun can head a nominal sentence; still no verb.

### 5. Prepositional phrase as predicate
**Read:** `ٱلْكِتَابُ فِي ٱلْبَيْتِ`
**Answer:** nominal. `ٱلْكِتَابُ` = mubtadaʾ. The predicate is the **jār-majrūr**
`فِي ٱلْبَيْتِ` ("in the house") — a preposition + its genitive noun standing in for "is in the
house." `فِي` puts `ٱلْبَيْتِ` in jarr (kasra). (Procedure:
[`../../nahw/procedures/idafa-jar-majrur.md`](../../nahw/procedures/idafa-jar-majrur.md).)

### 6. Iḍāfa (possessive construct) as one unit
**Read:** `بَابُ ٱلْبَيْتِ مَفْتُوحٌ`
**Answer:** nominal. The mubtadaʾ is the **iḍāfa** `بَابُ ٱلْبَيْتِ` ("the door of the house") —
two nouns, the second in jarr, no `الْ` on the first (a muḍāf cannot take `الْ`). `مَفْتُوحٌ` =
khabar ("[is] open"). Treat the iḍāfa as a single noun phrase. (See
[`../../nahw/procedures/idafa-jar-majrur.md`](../../nahw/procedures/idafa-jar-majrur.md).)

### 7. `إِنَّ` opens an emphatic nominal
**Read:** `إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌ`
**Answer:** nominal, headed by `إِنَّ` ("indeed"). After `إِنَّ` the subject `ٱللَّهَ` takes
**naṣb** (fatḥa) — it is now the *ism inna* — and `غَفُورٌ` is the *khabar inna* ("[is]
forgiving"). The fatḥa on `ٱللَّهَ` is governed by `إِنَّ`, not an object marker. (Particle:
[`../../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md`](../../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md).)

### 8. Negated verbal sentence
**Read:** `مَا ذَهَبَ ٱلْوَلَدُ`
**Answer:** verbal, negated. `مَا` = negation ("did not"), `ذَهَبَ` fiʿl, `ٱلْوَلَدُ` fāʿil →
"the boy did not go." Here `مَا` reads as negation because a past verb follows it in a
declarative frame. (`مَا` is multi-function — confirm the frame in
[`quranic-function-words.md`](quranic-function-words.md) and
[`../../nahw/procedures/negation.md`](../../nahw/procedures/negation.md).)

### 9. Prohibition vs. negation by the verb's mood
**Read:** `لَا تَحْزَنْ`
**Answer:** verbal, **prohibition**. `لَا` + a **jussive** (majzūm) verb `تَحْزَنْ` (sukūn
ending) = "do not grieve." Had the verb been indicative (`لَا تَحْزَنُ`), `لَا` would be plain
negation ("you do not grieve"). The **mood of the verb** decides which `لَا` you have.
(Procedure: [`../../nahw/procedures/negation.md`](../../nahw/procedures/negation.md).)

### 10. Question particle heads the clause
**Read:** `هَلْ تَعْلَمُ`
**Answer:** verbal, interrogative. `هَلْ` = a yes/no question marker ("do…?"), `تَعْلَمُ` =
verb ("you know") → "do you know?" The leading particle frames the whole clause as a question;
it is not the subject.

### 11. Relative clause modifies a noun
**Read:** `ٱلرَّجُلُ ٱلَّذِي عَلِمَ`
**Answer:** nominal noun phrase. `ٱلرَّجُلُ` ("the man") + `ٱلَّذِي` (relative pronoun, "who") +
`عَلِمَ` ("knew") → "the man who knew." `ٱلَّذِي` links a clause as a modifier; it agrees in
gender/number with its referent. (Referent logic:
[`../../nahw/procedures/referent-context.md`](../../nahw/procedures/referent-context.md).)

### 12. Apodosis: a conditional's two halves
**Read:** `مَنْ يَعْمَلْ خَيْرًا يَجِدْهُ`
**Answer:** conditional. `مَنْ` ("whoever") + the **condition** verb `يَعْمَلْ` (jussive) +
object `خَيْرًا` ("good"), then the **answer** verb `يَجِدْهُ` (jussive, + object pronoun "it")
→ "whoever does good will find it." Both verbs are jussive — the mark of a conditional pair.
(Procedure: [`../../nahw/procedures/conditionals.md`](../../nahw/procedures/conditionals.md).)

### 13. Prepositional phrase attachment
**Read:** `جَاءَ بِكِتَابٍ`
**Answer:** verbal. `جَاءَ` = fiʿl, and `بِكِتَابٍ` is a jār-majrūr attached to the verb frame.
The bā' is not decoration and the host is not enough by itself. If a PP could attach as a
predicate, hāl, ṣifa, or hidden complement and the frame does not decide, write
`pending: pp_attachment`.

## Checklist before you leave sentence foundations

- [ ] Do I name **nominal vs. verbal** from the first content word *before* translating?
- [ ] In a nominal sentence, can I split **mubtadaʾ** from **khabar** and supply the silent
      "is"?
- [ ] In a verbal sentence, do I expect the **doer after the verb**, and read **ḍamma=doer /
      fatḥa=object** from the case endings?
- [ ] Do I treat an **iḍāfa** and a **jār-majrūr** as single units (and remember a muḍāf takes
      no `الْ`)?
- [ ] When a prepositional phrase appears, can I state what it attaches to — verb, predicate,
      hāl, ṣifa, or `pending: pp_attachment`?
- [ ] Do I let the **following verb's mood** decide `لَا` (negation vs. prohibition) and spot a
      **conditional** by its two jussive verbs?
- [ ] When the frame is genuinely ambiguous, do I write **PENDING** and route to the matching
      nahw procedure?
