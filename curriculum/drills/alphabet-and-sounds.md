# Drill — alphabet & sounds (Level 1)

**Goal:** read the Arabic script *out loud* — letters, their short vowels (harakāt), sukūn,
shadda, tanwīn, and the sun/moon split — well enough to sound out any vowelled word before you
ask what it *means*. This is the first rung of the ladder in [`../README.md`](../README.md);
meaning waits until Level 2.

**Rule of the drill:** the marks *are* the word. Arabic writes the consonant skeleton on the
line and the short vowels above/below it; drop a mark and you have changed the word, not
decorated it. A learner who skips the harakāt is guessing, and the whole curriculum forbids the
guess — read every mark you see, and when a mark is **absent** (sukūn) that absence is also
information.

> No meaning is taught here. Every item is "say it correctly," not "what does it mean." The
> words are real fuṣḥā but you read them as *sound* first.

## What each mark does (the kit you need before item 1)

| mark | name | on `ب` | reads |
|---|---|---|---|
| ـَ | fatḥa | بَ | "ba" (short a) |
| ـِ | kasra | بِ | "bi" (short i) |
| ـُ | ḍamma | بُ | "bu" (short u) |
| ـْ | sukūn | بْ | "b" — no vowel, the consonant closes a syllable |
| ـّ | shadda | بّ | doubled consonant — hold/geminate it ("bb") |
| ـً ـٍ ـٌ | tanwīn (fatḥ/kasr/ḍamm) | بًا / بٍ / بٌ | "‑an / ‑in / ‑un" — final nunation, an indefinite marker |

Three letters double as **long vowels** when they carry no vowel of their own: `ا` (long ā),
`و` (long ū, after ḍamma), `ي` (long ī, after kasra). Distinguish the *long vowel* from the
*consonant* `و` ("w") / `ي` ("y") — the harakah before it decides which.

## Items (read aloud, then check)

### 1. Three harakāt on one letter
**Read:** `كَ` · `كِ` · `كُ`
**Answer:** "ka" · "ki" · "ku". One consonant `ك`, three short vowels. The mark, not the
letter, changes the sound.

### 2. Sukūn closes a syllable
**Read:** `مِنْ`
**Answer:** "min" — kasra on `م`, **sukūn** on `ن` (no vowel). Two letters, one closed
syllable. (You will meet this word as a particle in
[`quranic-function-words.md`](quranic-function-words.md); here it is only sound.)

### 3. Shadda = a doubled consonant
**Read:** `رَبَّ`
**Answer:** "rabba" — the shadda on `ب` doubles it; you hold the *b*. Without the shadda,
`رَبَ` would be "raba" — a different word. The shadda is never optional.

### 4. Long vowel vs. short vowel
**Read:** `قَالَ` vs. `قَلَ`
**Answer:** "qāla" (long ā — the `ا` after fatḥa stretches the vowel) vs. "qala" (short a, no
alif). The alif here is a **long vowel**, not a consonant.

### 5. `و` as consonant vs. long vowel
**Read:** `وَ` vs. `نُورْ`
**Answer:** "wa" (consonant `و` = "w", carries fatḥa) vs. "nūr" (the `و` after ḍamma is a
**long ū**). Same letter, two jobs — the preceding harakah tells you which.

### 6. `ي` as consonant vs. long vowel
**Read:** `يَدْ` vs. `فِيلْ`
**Answer:** "yad" (consonant `ي` = "y", carries fatḥa) vs. "fīl" (the `ي` after kasra is a
**long ī**).

### 7. Tanwīn — the three nunations
**Read:** `كِتَابًا` · `كِتَابٍ` · `كِتَابٌ`
**Answer:** "kitāban" · "kitābin" · "kitābun". The doubled final mark adds an *n* sound; it
marks an **indefinite** noun (no `الْ`). Note `ـًا` is written with a carrier alif but read
"‑an".

### 8. Sun letter — `الْ` assimilates
**Read:** `الشَّمْس`
**Answer:** "ash-shams" (not "al-shams"). Before a **sun letter** (`ت ث د ذ ر ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ل ن`)
the *l* of `الْ` is dropped in speech and the next letter is doubled — note the **shadda** on
`ش`. The `ل` is still written but not pronounced.

### 9. Moon letter — `الْ` stays clear
**Read:** `الْقَمَر`
**Answer:** "al-qamar." Before a **moon letter** (`ا ب ج ح خ ع غ ف ق ك م ه و ي`) the *l* is
pronounced fully — note the **sukūn** on `ل`, and no shadda on `ق`. Sun vs. moon is the
mnemonic source of the two names (shams = sun letter, qamar = moon letter).

### 10. Hamza is a consonant (a glottal stop)
**Read:** `سَأَلَ`
**Answer:** "saʾala" — the `ء` (here seated on alif: `أ`) is a real consonant, a catch in the
throat, not silent. It carries harakāt like any letter: here fatḥa.

### 11. Tāʾ marbūṭa — `ة`
**Read:** `مَدْرَسَةٌ` (in pause: `مَدْرَسَهْ`)
**Answer:** "madrasatun" when followed by an ending; "madrasah" when you stop on it. The `ة`
reads as *t* in continuation and as a soft *h* in pause. It typically marks a feminine noun —
a signal you will use in [`morphology-foundations.md`](morphology-foundations.md).

### 12. Shadda + harakah stack
**Read:** `يُعَلِّمُ`
**Answer:** "yuʿallimu" — `ع` has fatḥa, `ل` carries **both** a shadda (double the *l*) **and**
a kasra. Read the shadda first (double the letter), then the vowel on it: "‑lli‑".

### 13. Madda — the long-alif cap
**Read:** `آمَنَ`
**Answer:** "āmana." The `آ` (alif madda, `ـٰٔ` cap) is hamza + long ā fused into one sign —
read a glottal onset stretched into a long *a*. It is one syllable nucleus, not two alifs.

### 14. Alif maqṣūra — `ى`
**Read:** `هُدَى`
**Answer:** "hudā." The final `ى` (dotless yāʾ) is read as a **long ā**, not "y". It is a
spelling variant of final long ā; do not voice it as a consonant.

## Checklist before you leave Level 1

- [ ] Can I give the **three** short-vowel sounds for any single letter on sight?
- [ ] Do I read **sukūn** as "close the syllable, no vowel" rather than skipping it?
- [ ] Do I **double** every shadda letter, then add its own harakah?
- [ ] Can I tell `و`/`ي` as a **consonant** from `و`/`ي` as a **long vowel** by the harakah
      before it?
- [ ] Do I assimilate `الْ` on a **sun** letter (shadda, no *l*) and keep it on a **moon**
      letter (sukūn on `ل`)?
- [ ] Do I read `ة` as *t* in continuation, soft *h* in pause?

When you can read all 14 items aloud without hesitating, move to
[`quranic-function-words.md`](quranic-function-words.md) and
[`morphology-foundations.md`](morphology-foundations.md). Reading the script correctly is the
floor everything else stands on — **a misread mark is a wrong word.**
