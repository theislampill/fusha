# Drill — Qurʾānic function words (the particle pool)

**Goal:** recognize and gloss the **highest-frequency ḥurūf** — the prepositions, conjunctions,
negators, and relatives that glue almost every āyah together. A learner who knows the top ~20
function words on sight reads the *structure* of a verse before knowing a single content word.

**Rule of the drill:** read the **content letter, not the first letter**, and read its harakah /
hamza-seat / shadda. A `و`/`فـ`/`بـ` proclitic shifts the decisive mark one position right
(`وَمِنَ` is *and + from*, not *and + who*). These words are short, frequent, and the worst
homograph offenders — when the marks are missing or the frame is unclear, the answer is a
specific **`pending:`**, never a coin-flip.

> Each item cites an āyah **source-address** (`quran:S:A:W`, per
> [`../../qamus/reports/source-address-model.md`](../../qamus/reports/source-address-model.md))
> as the *position* where the learner meets the word, and gives the **safe authored gloss**
> (`{src:'qamus', kind:'authored'}`). The full disambiguator logic lives in
> [`../../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../../nahw/drills/particles.md) and
> [`../../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md`](../../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md);
> this drill is the learner-facing recognition pass over them. Qurʾān text is never altered —
> the address points at the verbatim word.

## Items (recognize → gloss)

### 1. `بِسْمِ` — `بِـ` (preposition) — `quran:1:1:1`
The bāʾ proclitic on `ٱسْمِ` ("name"). **Gloss:** "in the name of." Read it as `بـ` + a jarr
noun; the bāʾ carries the "in/with" sense and the noun after it sits in jarr (kasra).

### 2. `ٱللَّهِ` and the article `ٱلْ` — `quran:1:1:2`
The `ٱلْ` is the **definite article** "the" — but it is *part of the noun*, not its own hover
word. **Gloss:** fold into the noun (definiteness only); it never produces a standalone gloss.

### 3. `ٱلْحَمْدُ … لِلَّهِ` — `لِـ` (preposition) — `quran:1:2:2`
The lām proclitic = "to / for / belonging to." In `لِلَّهِ` → "to/for Allah." **Gloss:** "for,
to (belonging to)." The lām puts its noun in jarr.

### 4. `رَبِّ ٱلْعَالَمِينَ` — iḍāfa, no particle — `quran:1:2:3`
A reminder the learner does **not** invent a particle: `رَبِّ ٱلْعَالَمِينَ` ("Lord of the
worlds") is an **iḍāfa**, a noun-to-noun construct, with no preposition between them. **Gloss:**
"of" is *implied* by the construct, not a written word. (See
[`../../nahw/procedures/idafa-jar-majrur.md`](../../nahw/procedures/idafa-jar-majrur.md).)

### 5. `إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ` — `وَ` (conjunction) — `quran:1:5:3`
The wāw proclitic on `إِيَّاكَ`. **Gloss:** "and." Strip the wāw before reading the next word;
default sense is conjunction "and" (the oath/circumstantial senses need a specific frame — see
[`../../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../../nahw/drills/particles.md)).

### 6. `ٱهْدِنَا ٱلصِّرَٰطَ` — pronoun clitic `ـنَا` — `quran:1:6:1`
The suffix `ـنَا` ("us") attached to the verb `ٱهْدِ` ("guide"). **Gloss:** "us" (object
pronoun). Recognize that an attached pronoun is one of the most frequent "function" pieces; it
is not a separate word on the line. (Clitic table:
[`../../nahw/procedures/preposition-pronoun.md`](../../nahw/procedures/preposition-pronoun.md).)

### 7. `صِرَٰطَ ٱلَّذِينَ` — relative `ٱلَّذِينَ` — `quran:1:7:1`
The relative pronoun "those who" (masc. plural). **Gloss:** "those who." It heads a relative
clause modifying the noun before it; agrees in number/gender with its referent. (Referent:
[`../../nahw/procedures/referent-context.md`](../../nahw/procedures/referent-context.md).)

### 8. `لَا ٱلضَّآلِّينَ` — `لَا` (negation/coordination) — `quran:1:7:5`
Here, after `غَيْرِ`, the `لَا` coordinates a second excluded group → "and not / nor (the
astray)." **Gloss:** "nor." Note `لَا` is multi-function (plain negation, "there is no,"
prohibition); the **frame** decides — see
[`../../nahw/procedures/negation.md`](../../nahw/procedures/negation.md).

### 9. `ذَٰلِكَ ٱلْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ` — `فِي` (preposition) + `هِ` — `quran:2:2:6`
`فِيهِ` = `فِي` ("in") + clitic `ـهِ` ("it"). **Gloss:** "in it." `فِي` is unambiguous in
spelling (fāʾ + yāʾ); its noun/pronoun is in jarr.

### 10. `هُدًى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ` — `لِـ` to a definite noun — `quran:2:2:8`
`لِلْمُتَّقِينَ` = `لِـ` ("for") + `ٱلْمُتَّقِينَ` ("the God-conscious"). **Gloss:** "for the
God-conscious." Note how the article's alif elides after the lām (`لِلْ‑`) — read the lām as the
preposition and the rest as its noun.

### 11. `ٱلَّذِينَ يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ` — relative + `بِـ` — `quran:2:3:1`
Two function words in one phrase: `ٱلَّذِينَ` ("those who") + later `بِٱلْغَيْبِ` = `بِـ`
("in") + `ٱلْغَيْبِ` ("the unseen"). **Gloss:** "those who…" and "in the unseen." The bāʾ here
carries the verb's transitivity (`آمَنَ بِـ` "believe **in**") — its gloss fuses with the verb.

### 12. `وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَٰهُمْ` — `وَ` + `مِنْ` + `مَا` — `quran:2:3:5`
A proclitic stack: `وَ` ("and") + `مِنْ` ("from / of") + `مَا` ("that which") fused as
`وَمِمَّا`. **Gloss:** "and from that which." This is the canonical *strip-the-proclitic*
exercise — the content particles are `مِنْ` (kasra mīm = "from," **not** `مَنْ` "who") and
relative `مَا`. (Homograph guard:
[`../../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../../nahw/drills/particles.md).)

### 13. `هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ` — pronoun `هُمْ` (subject) — `quran:2:4:6`
The detached pronoun `هُمْ` ("they") heading an emphatic clause. **Gloss:** "they." Distinguish
the **detached** `هُمْ` (a standalone word) from the **clitic** `ـهُمْ` ("them," attached to a
noun/verb/preposition).

### 14. `إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ` — `إِنَّ` (emphasis) — `quran:2:20:17`
`إِنَّ` = hamza-on-alif with **kasra** seat + **shadda** on the nūn → emphatic "indeed."
**Gloss:** "indeed / verily." Distinguish by seat+shadda from `أَنَّ` (fatḥa seat = "that") and
`أَنْ` (fatḥa, no shadda = "to/that"). (Procedure:
[`../../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md`](../../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md).)

### 15. `عَلَىٰ هُدًى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ` — `عَلَىٰ` (preposition) — `quran:2:5:1`
`عَلَىٰ` (written with alif maqṣūra `ى`) = "on / upon." **Gloss:** "upon." In this phrase
"upon guidance." Its noun is in jarr; clitic forms (`عَلَيْهِ` "on/against him") shift the yāʾ
out. Root is `ʿ‑l‑w/y`, **not** anything else `norm()` might suggest.

### 16. `إِلَىٰ رَبِّهَا نَاظِرَةٌ` — `إِلَىٰ` (preposition) — `quran:75:23:1`
`إِلَىٰ` = "to / toward" (goal/terminus). **Gloss:** "to, toward." Critical guard: `إِلَىٰ`
and its clitic `إِلَيْهِ` ("to him") are root `ʾ‑l‑y` (the hamza seat is load-bearing); they are
**not** root `ل‑ي‑ن` — `norm()` drops the seat and creates that false match. Certify the seat.

## Checklist before you leave the function-word pool

- [ ] Do I **strip proclitics** (`وَ`, `فَـ`, `بِـ`, `لِـ`, `ٱلْ`) before reading the next
      word's harakah?
- [ ] Can I tell `مِنْ` ("from," kasra mīm) from `مَنْ` ("who," fatḥa/none) on sight?
- [ ] Do I separate a **detached** pronoun (`هُمْ` "they") from a **clitic** one (`ـهُمْ`
      "them")?
- [ ] Do I read `إِنَّ`/`أَنَّ`/`أَنْ` by **hamza seat + shadda**, not by the first glance?
- [ ] Do I guard `إِلَىٰ`/`إِلَيْهِ` against the false `ل‑ي‑ن` match (hamza seat kept)?
- [ ] When the marks are missing or the frame is unclear, do I emit a **specific `pending:`**
      (e.g. `pending: homograph_haraka`) instead of guessing?
