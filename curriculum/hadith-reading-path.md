# Ḥadīth reading path — al-Nawawī's Forty toward Ṣaḥīḥayn preparation

A reading **plan** for the learner who has cleared the Qurʾān function words
([`quran-reading-path.md`](quran-reading-path.md)) and is ready for classical **prose**. The
matn of An-Nawawī's Forty is short, canonical, and vocabulary-bounded — the ideal first prose
after the Book.

> **No ḥadīth TEXT is bundled, reproduced, altered, or quoted in this file.** This is a reading
> *plan*: it points the learner at the catalogue and the procedures and tells them what to
> study, in what order. It does **not** contain matn paragraphs, isnād, translations, or
> commentary. **No Ṣaḥīḥayn content is reproduced anywhere here** — Ṣaḥīḥayn remains a future,
> owner-gated expansion (see [`../corpora/sahihayn/PLAN.md`](../corpora/sahihayn/PLAN.md)). The
> matn is treated read-only and is never altered; any gloss that could reach a public artifact
> is `{src:'qamus', kind:'authored'}`, and **PENDING beats a wrong gloss**. See
> [`README.md`](README.md) and [`../provenance/source-boundaries.md`](../provenance/source-boundaries.md).
> This is a **language** study layer; the learner verifies meaning with a qualified teacher.

## Prerequisites (do not start here cold)

Clear these before opening any prose:

1. The Qurʾān function-word ladder — at least Steps 1–7 of
   [`quran-reading-path.md`](quran-reading-path.md): `بِـ`, `الْ`, `لِـ`, `مِنْ`/`مَنْ`,
   `مَا`, `لَا`/`لَمْ`, `إِنَّ`/`أَنَّ`/`أَنْ`, the oath `وَ`, `إِلَّا`, `إِذَا`, the apodosis
   `فَـ`, and the relative `الَّذِي`-family.
2. Iḍāfa and jār-majrūr as constructs —
   [`../nahw/procedures/idafa-jar-majrur.md`](../nahw/procedures/idafa-jar-majrur.md).
3. The two sentence types and the first-governor habit —
   [`../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md`](../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md) §1–§2.

Prose differs from the suwar you have read in three ways the plan targets directly: longer
sentences, a heavier **verb** load (Forms II–X, weak verbs), and a few register words that are
specific to ḥadīth reportage. Each step below names the **ḥadīth to read first** (by its place
in the Forty), the **vocabulary** to study via Qamus, and the **grammar procedures** that parse
it.

## How to read one ḥadīth (the loop, every time)

For each ḥadīth in the order below:

1. **Read the matn** in your own copy of An-Nawawī's Forty (a qualified printed or teacher-led
   edition). This plan does not reproduce the text.
2. **Vocabulary via Qamus** — for each content word, study its entry on `qamus.dawah.wiki`:
   root, form, senses, usage. Words already in the 2,092-entry core hover with an authored
   gloss; words specific to ḥadīth prose may be **candidates** not yet authored — those show a
   blank hover, and the catalogue at
   [`../corpora/nawawi40/nawawi40.summary.md`](../corpora/nawawi40/nawawi40.summary.md) tells
   you which bucket a word fell into (`already_in_qamus` vs a review-only candidate).
3. **Grammar via nahw procedures** — parse the sentence with the first-governor habit; resolve
   every particle by the harakah on its content letter, never by `norm()`.
4. **PENDING discipline** — for any word whose root, form, or sense you cannot certify, name the
   `pending:` reason and route to the procedure. Do not assert a root the catalogue itself left
   in the `new_root_or_unknown_root` bucket; that bucket is a **human/QAC** task, not a guess.

## Step 1 — Ḥadīth on intentions (innamā l-aʿmālu bi-l-niyyāt): the nominal sentence, restriction

**Read first.** The first ḥadīth of the Forty — the report on intentions. The owner's edition
supplies the matn; it is not reproduced here.

**Why first.** It is the shortest, most-quoted matn and is built on grammar you already own: a
nominal sentence with `إِنَّمَا` (restriction 'only / none but'), the preposition `بِـ` carrying
the predication, and a plain iḍāfa. No weak-verb traps, no rare register words.

**Vocabulary via Qamus.** Study the roots behind 'deed/work' (`ع م ل`) and 'intention'
(`ن و ي`, a **defective/weak** root — its final weak radical is the kind of thing
[`../sarf/procedures/weak-root.md`](../sarf/procedures/weak-root.md) recovers). Both lemmas sit
in the Qurʾān-centric core, so expect authored hovers.

**Grammar via nahw.**
- `إِنَّمَا` (restriction) — read as `إِنَّ` + `مَا` (kāffah): the **kasra** seat + **shadda**
  on the **ن** mark `إِنَّ`; the appended `مَا` neutralizes its governance and yields the
  'only' sense. Procedure: [`../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md`](../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md).
- Nominal sentence, mubtadaʾ + khabar via `بِـ`:
  [`../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md`](../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md) §1.

**Checkpoint.** Parse the opening clause as a **nominal** sentence, justify the 'only'
restriction from `إِنَّمَا`, and gloss the `بِـ` as predicative ('by / according to'), not as a
separate verb argument.

## Step 2 — Ḥadīth of Jibrīl (islām, īmān, iḥsān): definitions, the relative, listing wāw

**Read first.** The second ḥadīth — the report of the questioning by Jibrīl. (Matn in your
edition; not reproduced.)

**Why second.** Longer, but its grammar is a **list**: parallel definition clauses joined by
the conjunction `وَ` 'and', each a small nominal sentence. It drills the listing wāw against the
oath wāw you isolated in [`quran-reading-path.md`](quran-reading-path.md) Step 5 — here every
`وَ` is the conjunction (there is a governing structure), so the default 'and' holds.

**Vocabulary via Qamus.** The triad `إِسْلَام` / `إِيمَان` / `إِحْسَان` are **maṣādir** (Form IV
`إِفْعَال` and Form I), i.e. **nouns**, not verbs — do not let a verb gloss ('to …') attach.
**Guard:** `إِيمَان` 'faith' (root `أ م ن`) must never collapse onto `أَيْمَان` 'oaths' (root
`ي م ن`); the hamza seat is load-bearing and `norm()` drops it. Maṣdar classification:
[`../sarf/procedures/masdar-participle.md`](../sarf/procedures/masdar-participle.md); hamza-root
guard: [`../sarf/procedures/hamza-root.md`](../sarf/procedures/hamza-root.md).

**Grammar via nahw.**
- `أَنْ` (maṣdariyyah 'to / that' + subjunctive) in the definition clauses — distinguish from
  `أَنَّ` 'that' (+ noun clause) by the **shadda on the ن**:
  [`../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../nahw/drills/particles.md).
- Relative `مَا` / `الَّذِي` over the definition clauses:
  [`../nahw/references/quranic-nahw-notes.md`](../nahw/references/quranic-nahw-notes.md) §3.

**Checkpoint.** Classify each of the three head terms as a **maṣdar (noun)**, not a verb; and
for every `وَ` in the listing, confirm the conjunction reading over the oath reading.

## Step 3 — The five pillars ḥadīth (buniya l-islām ʿalā khams): the passive verb, counting

**Read first.** The third ḥadīth — the report that Islam is built upon five. (Matn not
reproduced.)

**Why third.** It introduces a **passive (mabnī lil-majhūl)** verb — 'was built' — and the
preposition `عَلَى` governing the list. Passive voice is the first real verb-morphology step
beyond the suwar.

**Vocabulary via Qamus.** Root 'to build' (`ب ن ي`, weak final radical) — **guard:** the noun
`ٱبْن` 'son' and `بَنَات` 'daughters' are from this root family but are **nouns**, not the verb
'to build'; class first. Counting nouns (`خَمْس` 'five') and their gender-reversal in numbers is
a recurring prose feature: [`../sarf/procedures/noun-plural-gender.md`](../sarf/procedures/noun-plural-gender.md).

**Grammar via nahw.**
- Passive verb recognition (the vowel pattern, not the root, marks the majhūl):
  [`../nahw/references/irab-case-mood.md`](../nahw/references/irab-case-mood.md) and
  [`../sarf/procedures/verb-form.md`](../sarf/procedures/verb-form.md).
- `عَلَى` (super-position) governing a jarr complement:
  [`../nahw/procedures/preposition-pronoun.md`](../nahw/procedures/preposition-pronoun.md).

**Checkpoint.** Identify the verb as **passive** from its vowelling (not from the root), and say
why `ٱبْن`/`بَنَات` get noun glosses while the finite verb here gets the verb gloss.

## Step 4 — The creation-in-the-womb ḥadīth: sequence, the verbal sentence, weak verbs

**Read first.** The fourth ḥadīth — the report on the stages of creation. (Matn not reproduced.)

**Why fourth.** It is a **narrative**: a chain of **verbal sentences** (verb-first), strung by
the sequential `فَـ` and `ثُمَّ` ('then'). It drills verb-first word order and a cluster of weak
verbs at narrative pace.

**Vocabulary via Qamus.** Several weak verbs surface here (hollow and defective). Recover each
third/middle radical from the entry, never from the surface alif:
[`../sarf/procedures/weak-root.md`](../sarf/procedures/weak-root.md),
[`../sarf/references/weak-verbs.md`](../sarf/references/weak-verbs.md). Many of these are core
Qurʾānic verbs with authored hovers; the catalogue marks the ones still in review.

**Grammar via nahw.**
- Verbal sentence (fiʿl + fāʿil + mafʿūl) and reading the doer/done-to roles:
  [`../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md`](../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md) §2.
- `ثُمَّ` / `فَـ` as sequence markers (surface gloss 'then'):
  [`../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../nahw/drills/particles.md).

**Checkpoint.** For three consecutive narrative clauses, name the verb, its doer, and its
object, in that order; and recover at least one weak verb's underlying radical from its Qamus
entry rather than from the surface `ا`/`ى`.

## Step 5 — Onward through the Forty: the register words, and where the plan stops

After Steps 1–4 the learner has met the core prose machinery — nominal and verbal sentences,
the passive, weak verbs at pace, restriction, listing, sequence. The remaining ḥadīth of the
Forty reinforce these and add a small set of **ḥadīth-register** words (transmission verbs and
reportage formulae) that are *low priority* for a Qurʾān-centric Qamus and are catalogued
separately.

**Vocabulary via Qamus, with honest coverage.** The Nawawī40 catalogue
([`../corpora/nawawi40/nawawi40.summary.md`](../corpora/nawawi40/nawawi40.summary.md)) measured
**394** of the Forty's distinct lemmas as already in the 2,092-entry core (expect authored
hovers there), with the rest as **review-only candidates** — including a
`new_root_or_unknown_root` bucket dominated by reportage forms (e.g. the verb 'narrated it',
root `ر و ي`). Those candidates **hover blank** until a human authors them; that blank is
correct, not a gap to paper over. The catalogue is review-only and writes **nothing** live:
candidates flow through [`../qamus/reports/fusha-to-qamus-highlight-bridge.md`](../qamus/reports/fusha-to-qamus-highlight-bridge.md),
human-gated.

**The register's PENDING rule.** The catalogue deliberately left the weak/unknown roots
**un-asserted** (a prototype auto-tie measured only ~50% precision). The learner inherits the
same discipline: a reportage word whose root you cannot certify gets a `pending:` and a route to
[`../sarf/procedures/root-decision.md`](../sarf/procedures/root-decision.md), not a confident
guess.

**Checkpoint (prose gate).** Read an unseen ḥadīth from the Forty and, for each content word,
produce root/form/POS, the role its governor assigns, and a cited gloss — **or** the most
specific `pending:` reason. You pass when no word carries a confident wrong reading. This is the
prose analogue of the fluency gate in
[`../nahw/curriculum/zero-to-fluency-nahw.md`](../nahw/curriculum/zero-to-fluency-nahw.md)
Stage 7.

## Toward Ṣaḥīḥayn — a pointer, not a path

Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī and Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim are the natural next horizon, but they are **not** part of
this plan and **no Ṣaḥīḥayn text is staged, tokenized, quoted, or committed** anywhere in this
repo. Expansion is **owner-gated** and, when authorized, would reuse the same catalogue-first,
review-only pipeline that the Nawawī40 pilot proved — matn-only, isnād excluded, vocabulary
cataloguing only, no text redistribution. The roadmap and its hard constraints live in
[`../corpora/sahihayn/PLAN.md`](../corpora/sahihayn/PLAN.md). Until that gate opens, the Forty
**is** the prose ceiling of this curriculum.

**What the learner can do now, honestly:** finish the Forty with the loop above; for each new
word, study it on `qamus.dawah.wiki` and respect the blank hover; build the prose-parsing reflex
on a small, canonical, well-bounded text so that — *if and when* a larger corpus is authorized
and its vocabulary authored — the reading skill is already in place.
