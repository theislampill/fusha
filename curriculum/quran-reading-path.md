# Qurʾān reading path — al-Fātiḥa first, then the short suwar

A **graded** reading ladder for the ʿajamī learner who can read the script and knows the
core particles. Each step names: the **āyāt** to read (token-addressed `quran:S:A:W`), the
**function words** that step teaches, and the **drill** that trains them. You read on
`qamus.dawah.wiki` with the hover layer on: hover or tap a Qurʾānic word and, when it is
covered, an **authored** English gloss appears (`{src:'qamus', kind:'authored'}`); when it is
not covered, the hover is **blank** — that blank is honest, and your move is to run the
procedure, not to guess.

> Qurʾān text is never altered here; words appear only as text to be read, addressed by
> position (`quran:S:A:W`), never edited for a drill. External corpora are internal evidence
> only; the public render carries only `{src:'qamus', kind:'authored'}`. See
> [`README.md`](README.md) and [`../provenance/source-boundaries.md`](../provenance/source-boundaries.md).
> This is a **language** study layer: verify meaning against the muṣḥaf and a qualified teacher.

## How to climb

For every step: **(1)** read the procedure for that step's function words, **(2)** do the
named drill, **(3)** read the āyāt with the hover layer, **(4)** for any word whose hover is
blank or whose harakah you cannot resolve, say the precise `pending:` reason aloud rather than
inventing a gloss. The whole skill is **stop guessing**: when the evidence does not decide,
`pending` *is* the right answer.

The ladder is ordered by **function-word load**, not by sūrah number — al-Fātiḥa and the very
short late-Meccan suwar carry the smallest closed set of particles, so they come first.

---

## Step 1 — al-Fātiḥa, the opening: `بِـ`, `الْ`, `لِـ`, iḍāfa

**Read.** `quran:1:1:1`–`quran:1:7:end` (the seven āyāt). Start with the basmala
`بِسْمِ ٱللَّهِ ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ` `quran:1:1:1`–`quran:1:1:4`.

**Function words to learn.**
- `بِـ` (proclitic preposition) — here `بِسْمِ` 'in the name of'; its object is **jarr**.
- `الْ` (definite article) — folds into its noun, no standalone gloss; marks definiteness.
- `لِـ` (lām) — `ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ` `quran:1:2:1`–`quran:1:2:2` 'praise belongs **to** Allah'.
- **iḍāfa** — `رَبِّ ٱلْعَٰلَمِينَ` `quran:1:2:3` 'Lord **of** the worlds' (definite by the
  second term); `مَٰلِكِ يَوْمِ ٱلدِّينِ` `quran:1:4:1`–`quran:1:4:3` 'Master of the Day of
  Judgement' — a two-link iḍāfa.

**Drills.** Particles: [`../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../nahw/drills/particles.md) (`بِـ`, `الْ`).
Construct + preposition: [`../nahw/drills/idafa-and-jar-majrur.md`](../nahw/drills/idafa-and-jar-majrur.md).
Procedures: [`../nahw/procedures/idafa-jar-majrur.md`](../nahw/procedures/idafa-jar-majrur.md),
[`../nahw/procedures/preposition-pronoun.md`](../nahw/procedures/preposition-pronoun.md).

**Checkpoint.** In `إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ` `quran:1:5:1`–`quran:1:5:4`,
identify `وَ` as the conjunction 'and' (not the oath wāw — there is a governing verb), and say
why `إِيَّاكَ` is an object pronoun, not a verb.

---

## Step 2 — al-Ikhlāṣ, pure tawḥīd: `هُوَ`, `أَحَد`, the negating `لَمْ`

**Read.** `quran:112:1:1`–`quran:112:4:end` (four āyāt).
`قُلْ هُوَ ٱللَّهُ أَحَدٌ` `quran:112:1:1`–`quran:112:1:3`.

**Function words to learn.**
- `هُوَ` (separate pronoun 'He') — mubtadaʾ of a nominal sentence; `quran:112:1:2`.
- `لَمْ` (negation + jussive) — `لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ` `quran:112:3:1`–`quran:112:3:4`:
  `لَمْ` negates the **past** ('did not beget … nor was begotten') even though the verb wears
  present form. The mood is **jazm** (`يَلِدْ`, sukūn) — read it back to confirm `لَمْ`.
- `لَهُ` (`لِـ` + `ـهُ`) — `وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌۢ` `quran:112:4:1`–`quran:112:4:5`
  'and there is **for Him** no equal'.

**Drills.** Negation: [`../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../nahw/drills/particles.md) (`لَا`/`لَمْ`).
Procedure: [`../nahw/procedures/negation.md`](../nahw/procedures/negation.md).
The `لَمْ` vs `لِمَ` harakah split: [`../nahw/references/particles.md`](../nahw/references/particles.md).

**Checkpoint.** State the tense `لَمْ` produces and the mood it forces. If you met `لم` with no
diacritics, you say `pending: homograph_haraka` (it could be `لِمَ` 'why').

---

## Step 3 — al-Falaq & al-Nās, seeking refuge: `مِنْ`, the relative `مَا`, `مَنْ` guard

**Read.** al-Falaq `quran:113:1:1`–`quran:113:5:end`; al-Nās `quran:114:1:1`–`quran:114:6:end`.
`قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ ٱلْفَلَقِ` `quran:113:1:1`–`quran:113:1:4`.

**Function words to learn.**
- `مِنْ` (preposition 'from') — `مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ` `quran:113:2:1`–`quran:113:2:3` 'from the
  evil of what He created'. **Guard:** this is `مِنْ` (kasra on the **م**, no shadda on the
  **ن**) — *not* `مَنْ` 'who'. Read the content letter, not the first letter.
- relative `مَا` ('that which / what') — `مَا خَلَقَ` `quran:113:2:2`–`quran:113:2:3` 'what He
  created'; `مَا` fills a noun slot and heads a clause.
- `إِذَا` (temporal/conditional 'when') — `مِن شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ` `quran:113:3:1`–
  `quran:113:3:4` 'when it settles'.

**Drills.** The `مِنْ`/`مَنْ` homograph: [`../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../nahw/drills/particles.md)
(use `is_man_who`). The many faces of `مَا`: same file, the `مَا` table.
Procedure: [`../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md`](../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md).

**Checkpoint.** For `مِن شَرِّ مَا` give each token: `مِنْ` = 'from', `شَرِّ` = 'evil of'
(muḍāf, jarr), `مَا` = 'what / that which'. If `ما` were genuinely frame-ambiguous you would
emit `pending: multi_sense` — but here the noun-slot frame decides it.

---

## Step 4 — al-Kawthar, the shortest sūrah: `إِنَّ`, `فَـ` of result, the imperative

**Read.** `quran:108:1:1`–`quran:108:3:end` (three āyāt).
`إِنَّآ أَعْطَيْنَٰكَ ٱلْكَوْثَرَ` `quran:108:1:1`–`quran:108:1:3`.

**Function words to learn.**
- `إِنَّ` (emphatic 'indeed / verily') — here fused as `إِنَّآ` (`إِنَّ` + `ـنا`); the **kasra**
  on the hamza seat **plus** the **shadda** on the **ن** mark it `إِنَّ`, distinct from `أَنْ`
  'to/that' (fatḥa seat, no shadda) and `أَنَّ` 'that' (fatḥa seat, shadda).
- `فَـ` (sequential / result fāʾ 'so, then') — `فَصَلِّ لِرَبِّكَ وَٱنْحَرْ`
  `quran:108:2:1`–`quran:108:2:4` 'so pray to your Lord and sacrifice'; `فَـ` here draws the
  result of āyah 1.

**Drills.** `إِنَّ`/`أَنَّ`/`أَنْ`/`إِنْ` (seat + shadda): [`../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../nahw/drills/particles.md);
deeper worked pairs in [`../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md`](../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md).
The `فَـ` of result and of apodosis: [`../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../nahw/drills/particles.md).
Procedure: [`../nahw/procedures/relative-interrogative.md`](../nahw/procedures/relative-interrogative.md).

**Checkpoint.** Show, with the seat and shadda, why `إِنَّآ` is `إِنَّ` and not `أَنَّ`; then say
which clause `فَـ` opens (the result of the gift in āyah 1).

---

## Step 5 — al-ʿAṣr, the wāw al-qasam (oath wāw): `وَ` as 'by', `إِلَّا`, the exceptive

**Read.** `quran:103:1:1`–`quran:103:3:end` (three āyāt).
`وَٱلْعَصْرِ ۝ إِنَّ ٱلْإِنسَٰنَ لَفِى خُسْرٍ` `quran:103:1:1`–`quran:103:2:4`.

**Function words to learn.**
- `وَ` as **oath** ('by') — `وَٱلْعَصْرِ` `quran:103:1:1` 'by the ʿaṣr'. This is the wāw
  al-qasam: clause-initial, the following noun is **jarr**, and there is **no governing verb**.
  Contrast the conjunction `وَ` 'and' you met in Step 1; the default is 'and', and you flag
  the oath sense only on this exact frame.
- `لَـ` of emphasis + `فِي` — `لَفِى خُسْرٍ` `quran:103:2:3`–`quran:103:2:4` 'surely in loss'.
- `إِلَّا` (exception 'except / but') — `إِلَّا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا` `quran:103:3:1`–
  `quran:103:3:2` 'except those who believed'. **Guard:** `إِلَّا` has a **shadda on the lām**
  and a hamza seat — do not confuse it with `لَا` (neither).
- `ٱلَّذِينَ` (relative pronoun, masc. pl. 'those who') — heads a ṣilah clause; gloss as a
  whole, never broken to a root.

**Drills.** Oath vs conjunction wāw: [`../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../nahw/drills/particles.md)
(`وَ` table). `إِلَّا` vs `لَا`: same file. Relatives: [`../nahw/references/quranic-nahw-notes.md`](../nahw/references/quranic-nahw-notes.md) §3.
Procedure: [`../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md`](../nahw/procedures/particle-decision.md).

**Checkpoint.** Justify the oath reading of `وَٱلْعَصْرِ` from its three cues (clause-initial,
jarr noun, no verb), and parse `إِلَّا ٱلَّذِينَ` as exceptive + relative.

---

## Step 6 — al-Kāfirūn, negation drilled: `لَا`, `مَا`, the nominal sentence

**Read.** `quran:109:1:1`–`quran:109:6:end` (six āyāt).
`قُلْ يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلْكَٰفِرُونَ ۝ لَآ أَعْبُدُ مَا تَعْبُدُونَ` `quran:109:1:1`–`quran:109:2:3`.

**Function words to learn.**
- `لَا` (simple negation 'not') — `لَآ أَعْبُدُ` `quran:109:2:1`–`quran:109:2:2` 'I do not
  worship'; the following verb is **not** jussive, so this is *nāfiyah* ('not'), not *nāhiyah*
  ('do not').
- relative `مَا` (for non-persons) — `مَا تَعْبُدُونَ` `quran:109:2:2`–`quran:109:2:3` 'what
  you worship'. Note `مَا` here is **relative**, not the negating `مَا`; the frame (filling the
  object slot) decides.
- `وَلَا` (coordinating 'and not / nor') — `وَلَآ أَنتُمْ عَٰبِدُونَ` `quran:109:3:3`–
  `quran:109:3:5` 'nor are you worshippers'.

**Drills.** `لَا` four functions (by the following verb's mood): [`../nahw/drills/particles.md`](../nahw/drills/particles.md).
The heaviest particle `مَا`: same file. Procedure:
[`../nahw/procedures/negation.md`](../nahw/procedures/negation.md).

**Checkpoint.** Distinguish, in this sūrah, `لَا` (negation, present verb follows) from a `مَا`
that is **relative** ('what'), not negating. If a `لَا` is followed by an unmarked verb whose
mood you cannot see → `pending: context` ('not' vs 'do not').

---

## Step 7 — al-Naṣr, conditionals: `إِذَا`, the apodosis `فَـ`, the imperative chain

**Read.** `quran:110:1:1`–`quran:110:3:end` (three āyāt).
`إِذَا جَآءَ نَصْرُ ٱللَّهِ وَٱلْفَتْحُ` `quran:110:1:1`–`quran:110:1:5`.

**Function words to learn.**
- `إِذَا` (temporal conditional 'when / if', more certain) — heads the *šarṭ* (protasis);
  `quran:110:1:1`.
- apodosis `فَـ` (fāʾ al-jawāb 'then') — `فَسَبِّحْ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّكَ`
  `quran:110:3:1`–`quran:110:3:3` 'then glorify your Lord with His praise'; this `فَـ` marks
  where the *jawāb* (answer) begins. Surface gloss stays 'then'.
- `كَانَ` (the verb 'to be') — `إِنَّهُۥ كَانَ تَوَّابَۢا` `quran:110:3:5`–`quran:110:3:7`
  'indeed He is ever Accepting of repentance'.

**Drills.** Conditional structure (šarṭ / jawāb / apodosis fāʾ):
[`../nahw/references/quranic-nahw-notes.md`](../nahw/references/quranic-nahw-notes.md) §2;
worked frames in [`../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md`](../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md).
Procedure: [`../nahw/procedures/conditionals.md`](../nahw/procedures/conditionals.md).

**Checkpoint.** Mark the two halves of the conditional: the šarṭ opened by `إِذَا`
(`quran:110:1:1`) and the jawāb opened by the apodosis `فَـ` (`quran:110:3:1`). Explain why the
`فَـ` here is *not* the result fāʾ of Step 4 but the answer of a condition.

---

## Step 8 — al-Māʿūn, sentence type & the referent guard: `الَّذِي`, `هُمْ`

**Read.** `quran:107:1:1`–`quran:107:7:end` (seven āyāt).
`أَرَءَيْتَ ٱلَّذِى يُكَذِّبُ بِٱلدِّينِ` `quran:107:1:1`–`quran:107:1:4`.

**Function words to learn.**
- interrogative-ish opener `أَرَءَيْتَ` ('have you seen?') + relative `ٱلَّذِي` ('the one who')
  — `quran:107:1:2`–`quran:107:1:3`.
- `فَـ` of consequence introducing the *fāʾ + ḏālika* idiom — `فَذَٰلِكَ ٱلَّذِى يَدُعُّ
  ٱلْيَتِيمَ` `quran:107:2:1`–`quran:107:2:4` 'for that is the one who drives away the orphan'.
- the pronoun `هُمْ` and `ٱلَّذِينَ` over a relative clause — `فَوَيْلٌ لِّلْمُصَلِّينَ ۝
  ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَن صَلَاتِهِمْ سَاهُونَ` `quran:107:4:1`–`quran:107:5:4`. Note `هُمْ` is the
  mubtadaʾ of the inner nominal clause; the **referent guard** keeps `هُمْ`/`ـهِمْ` tracking the
  same antecedent (the heedless worshippers).

**Drills.** Nominal vs verbal sentence, first governor:
[`../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md`](../nahw/drills/sentence-context.md) §1–§2. Relatives and
the referent guard: [`../nahw/references/quranic-nahw-notes.md`](../nahw/references/quranic-nahw-notes.md)
§3–§4. Procedure: [`../nahw/procedures/referent-context.md`](../nahw/procedures/referent-context.md).

**Checkpoint (reading gate).** Take this whole sūrah and, for each āyah, name the sentence type,
the first governor, and for every pronoun the antecedent it points back to. Where a clitic's
English (it/him/them) depends on an unresolved antecedent → `pending: referent_unresolved`.

**The through-line.** Eight short suwar, eight tight bundles of function words, each addressed
