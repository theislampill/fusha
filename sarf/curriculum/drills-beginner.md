# Drills — beginner ṣarf

The first rung of the curriculum ([`zero-to-fluency-sarf.md`](zero-to-fluency-sarf.md),
stages 1–2). Two skills only: **(A) recover the three radicals** and **(B) match a surface to
its wazn and name verb-vs-noun.** Nothing here needs a derived form or a weak-root recovery —
those come in [`drills-intermediate.md`](drills-intermediate.md).

Every drill is generated from a **real Qamus root** and cites the **source-address node** it
came from (the graph in [`../../qamus/reports/source-address-model.md`](../../qamus/reports/source-address-model.md)).
Cover the answer, decide, then check — the **reasoning** is the part that matters; a right
answer for the wrong reason will fail you at the intermediate stage.

> The one rule that outranks all others here: **`norm()` proposes, harakāt/QAC certify.** When
> a drill says "you see the surface", the surface is evidence, not proof — confirm against the
> cited entry. See [`../procedures/root-decision.md`](../procedures/root-decision.md).

---

## Part A — recover the three radicals

The radicals are the consonants that carry the meaning. Strip any proclitic (`وَ فَ الْ بِ
كَ لِ`) and any enclitic pronoun (`ـهُ ـهَا ـنَا ـكُمْ`), then read what is left. In a **sound**
root (no weak letter, no hamza, no doubling) all three radicals are visible — that is the case
this part drills.

### A1 — `كَسَبَ`  ·  src `qamus:v147#root=ك س ب`

- **Decide:** the three radicals?
- **Answer:** `ك س ب`. **Reasoning:** a clean `فَعَلَ` skeleton — `كَ-سَ-بَ` — every consonant
  is a radical, no affix, no weak letter. Certifies at rung 1 against the entry's root.

### A2 — `يَصْدُر`  ·  src `qamus:v188#root=ص د ر`

- **Decide:** the radicals, and what is the `يَـ`?
- **Answer:** `ص د ر`. The leading `يَـ` is the **imperfect prefix** (3rd-person masc.), **not**
  a radical. **Reasoning:** strip the muḍāriʿ prefix `يـ`; `صْ-دُ-ر` remains. Reading `ي` as a
  radical would invent the bogus root `ي ص د`.

### A3 — `وَكَسَبَ`  ·  src `qamus:v147#root=ك س ب`

- **Decide:** radicals?
- **Answer:** `ك س ب`. The opening `وَ` is the **conjunction "and"** (a proclitic), not a
  radical. **Reasoning:** strip `وَ` first → `كَسَبَ` → `ك س ب`. The `و` here is *not* the weak
  first radical of an assimilated root; context (a leading "and") tells you it is the clitic.

### A4 — `بَغْضَاء`  ·  src `qamus:n300#root=ب غ ض`

- **Decide:** radicals? (and is the final `ـَاء` a radical?)
- **Answer:** `ب غ ض`. The `ـَاء` is the **`فَعْلَاء` pattern tail** (an extension marking the
  noun), not a fourth radical. **Reasoning:** three radicals `ب-غ-ض`; the long `ـَاء` is wazn,
  not root. This is a noun ("intense hatred"), so see Part B before any gloss.

### A5 — `زَاغَ`  ·  src `qamus:v509#root=ز ي غ`

- **Decide:** radicals?
- **Answer:** `ز ي غ`. **Reasoning — read carefully:** the surface shows only `ز ا غ`, but the
  cited root is `ز ي غ`. The medial `ا` is the **weak middle radical `ي` surfacing as a long
  ā** (a *hollow* verb). You could not have *certified* `ز ي غ` from the surface alone — the
  entry/QAC does. (Full mechanism at Stage 5; here just note the trap: not every `ا` is a
  radical you can read off.)

### A6 — `خَفَّت`  ·  src `qamus:v379#root=خ ف ف`

- **Decide:** how many radicals, and what is the shadda doing?
- **Answer:** three — `خ ف ف`. The **shadda on the `ف` is two identical radicals merged**
  (C2 = C3). **Reasoning:** expand the shadda before counting; `خَفّ` = `خ-ف-ف`, a *doubled*
  (geminate) root, not the bi-consonantal `خ ف` it looks like. The final `ـت` is the feminine
  suffix on the verb, not a radical.

## Part B — match the surface to its wazn (verb or noun?)

The **wazn** (pattern) decides the *kind* of word before you ever pick English. The single
most important question at this stage: **verb or noun?** — because a noun never takes a "to …"
gloss. (Class → gloss-vocabulary; see
[`../references/quranic-morphology-notes.md`](../references/quranic-morphology-notes.md) §7.)

### B1 — `كَسَبَ`  ·  src `qamus:v147`

- **Decide:** wazn? verb or noun?
- **Answer:** wazn `فَعَلَ` → **Form I verb** (perfect). **Reasoning:** the `فَعَلَ` mould is a
  finite past-tense verb; English is finite — "he earned / acquired", never "to earn".

### B2 — `يَصْدُر`  ·  src `qamus:v188`

- **Decide:** wazn? verb or noun?
- **Answer:** wazn `يَفْعُل` → **Form I verb** (imperfect). **Reasoning:** the `يَـ` prefix +
  `ـُ` on C2 is the present tense — "he proceeds / issues forth". The same entry also carries
  the noun `صَدْر` ("chest") on the *same* root — different wazn, different class. Pattern, not
  root, tells them apart.

### B3 — `صَدْر`  ·  src `qamus:v188#root=ص د ر`

- **Decide:** wazn? verb or noun?
- **Answer:** wazn `فَعْل` → **noun** ("chest / breast"). **Reasoning:** `فَعْل` (fatḥa-sukūn)
  with no verb prefix is a noun shape. Same root as B2, but a **noun gloss only** — never "to
  proceed". This is the textbook same-root verb/noun split.

### B4 — `بَغْضَاء`  ·  src `qamus:n300`

- **Decide:** wazn? verb or noun?
- **Answer:** wazn `فَعْلَاء` → **noun** (a maṣdar-type "intense hatred / enmity").
  **Reasoning:** the `ـَاء` tail on a triliteral is a noun pattern; class `n` in the address
  confirms it. A "to hate" gloss here is a POS-mismatch defect.

### B5 — `شَطْأَهُ`  ·  src `qamus:n621#root=`

- **Decide:** strip the enclitic; what is left; verb or noun?
- **Answer:** `شَطْأ` + enclitic `ـهُ` ("its") → **noun** ("its shoot / sprout"). **Reasoning:**
  the final `ـهُ` is the 3rd-person possessive pronoun, not part of the word; `شَطْء` is a noun
  (class `n`). Never read `شطأه` as a verb; the `ـه` is a suffix, and the base is nominal.

### B6 — `مُلْك` vs `مَلِك` vs `مَلَك`  ·  root `م ل ك` (see [`../drills/root-detection.md`](../drills/root-detection.md) ex. 5)

- **Decide:** one root, three surfaces — name each wazn and meaning.
- **Answer:** `مُلْك` (`فُعْل`) "dominion" — **noun, maṣdar**; `مَلِك` (`فَعِل`) "king" —
  **noun**; `مَلَك` (`فَعَل`) "angel" — **noun**. **Reasoning:** identical consonants `م-ل-ك`;
  the **harakāt alone** select the word. `norm()` flattens all three to one key, so a root
  match licenses *no* gloss here — certify the sense from harakāt + context. Never gloss
  `ٱلْمُلْك` "angels".

## Checklist before you call a beginner drill "done"

- [ ] Did I **strip clitics** (`وَ فَ الْ بِ كَ لِ` / `ـهُ ـهَا ـنَا ـكُمْ`) before counting
      radicals?
- [ ] Did I **expand any shadda** (it may hide a doubled radical) before counting?
- [ ] Did I treat the muḍāriʿ prefix (`يَـ/تَـ/نَـ/أَـ`) as an affix, not a radical?
- [ ] For a noun shape, did I withhold any **"to …"** gloss? (class gates vocabulary)
- [ ] Where the surface couldn't certify the root (A5 hollow), did I **defer to the entry/QAC**
      rather than read it off?
- [ ] Public emit would be `{src:'qamus', kind:'authored'}` or nothing — no external prose.

When the harakāt are the only distinguisher and you cannot read them: **PENDING beats wrong.**
Advance to [`drills-intermediate.md`](drills-intermediate.md) only after Part A and Part B are
automatic.
