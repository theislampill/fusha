# Drills — intermediate naḥw

Trains Stages 3–5 of [`zero-to-fluency-nahw.md`](zero-to-fluency-nahw.md): **iḍāfa** (genitive
construct), **jār-majrūr** (preposition phrase role), **negation type** (which negative, what
tense/polarity), and **relative vs. interrogative** (`مَا`/`مَنْ`/`أَيّ`). Each item cites its
āyah by address (`quran:S:A:W`); the Qurʾān text is evidence only and never altered (see
[`../../qamus/reports/source-address-model.md`](../../qamus/reports/source-address-model.md)).

**Procedure.** (1) Find the **first governor**; (2) let it assign POS, case, and mood; (3) gloss
the *construct/phrase relationship*, not the bare head; (4) when the frame is genuinely
ambiguous, emit the **most specific `pending:` reason** rather than the commoner reading.
Ambiguous iḍāfa / jār-majrūr boundaries and multi-function `مَا` carry a **two-vote** gate
([`../procedures/grammar-risk-gate.md`](../procedures/grammar-risk-gate.md)).

---

## §1 — Iḍāfa: definiteness and the relationship come from the second term

**I1.** `رَبِّ ٱلْعَٰلَمِينَ` — render the construct; where is the definiteness? *(`quran:1:2:2`)*
- **Procedure:** head `رَبِّ` loses tanwīn; second term `ٱلْعَٰلَمِينَ` (definite by `ال`,
  jarr) supplies definiteness + relationship.
- **Answer:** 'Lord of the worlds' — definite. Never 'a lord'.

**I2.** `عِندَ رَبِّهِمْ` — what does the ẓarf `عِندَ` head? *(`quran:3:169:5`)*
- **Procedure:** `عِندَ` is a ẓarf heading an iḍāfa of place/relation; gloss the relation.
- **Answer:** 'with their Lord / in the presence of their Lord' — not bare 'near'. `رَبِّ` is
  muḍāf to the pronoun `ـهِمْ`.

**I3.** `حَبْلِ ٱللَّهِ` — definite or indefinite, and why? *(`quran:3:103:4`)*
- **Procedure:** head `حَبْلِ` has no tanwīn/`ال`; second term `ٱللَّهِ` is inherently definite.
- **Answer:** **definite** — 'the rope of Allah'. A head in iḍāfa to a definite term is itself
  definite; do not gloss it as 'a rope'.

**I4.** `يَوْمِ ٱلدِّينِ` — the relationship? *(`quran:1:4:2`)*
- **Answer:** 'the Day of Judgement / Recompense' — iḍāfa of specification; `يَوْمِ` (jarr after
  `مَٰلِكِ`) is itself muḍāf to `ٱلدِّينِ`. Chained iḍāfa: `مَٰلِكِ يَوْمِ ٱلدِّينِ`.

## §2 — Jār-majrūr: gloss the phrase role, the noun is "of …"

**I5.** `بِٱلْغَيْبِ` (e.g. `يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْغَيْبِ`) — phrase role + gloss. *(`quran:2:3:2`)*
- **Answer:** jār-majrūr; `بِـ` carries the verb's transitivity (`آمَنَ بِـ` 'believe **in**'),
  object `ٱلْغَيْبِ` in jarr → 'in the unseen'. The bi- folds into the verb's gloss.

**I6.** `عَلَىٰ هُدًى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ` — parse the two phrases. *(`quran:2:5:1`)*
- **Answer:** `عَلَىٰ هُدًى` 'upon guidance' (jār-majrūr, predicate); `مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ` 'from
  their Lord' (a second jār-majrūr modifying `هُدًى`). `مِّن` = kasra on the **م** → 'from'.

**I7.** `لَهُم مَّغْفِرَةٌ` — what is the syntactic role of `لَهُم`? *(`quran:8:74:9`)*
- **Answer:** fronted jār-majrūr serving as **khabar muqaddam** (preposed predicate) →
  'for them is forgiveness'; `مَّغْفِرَةٌ` (rafʿ) is the delayed mubtadaʾ. The lām = 'for'.

## §3 — Negation type: which negative, what tense/polarity

**I8.** `لَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ` — name the negation and its tense. *(`quran:112:4:1`)*
- **Procedure:** `لَمْ` + jussive negates the **past**.
- **Answer:** `لَمْ` 'there has not been / was not' — `يَكُن` is present-form, jussive, past
  meaning. 'And there is none comparable to Him.'

**I9.** `لَن تَنَالُوا ٱلْبِرَّ` — which negative, what time? *(`quran:3:92:1`)*
- **Answer:** `لَنْ` + **subjunctive** (`تَنَالُوا`) → categorical **future** negation: 'you
  will never attain'. Not simple 'not'.

**I10.** `لَا تَحْزَنْ` — negation or prohibition? *(`quran:9:40:11`)*
- **Procedure:** `لَا` + **jussive** = prohibition (lā al-nāhiyah).
- **Answer:** prohibition → **'do not grieve'**. If the following verb were indicative (rafʿ),
  `لَا` would be simple negation 'does not'. The **mood decides**.

**I11.** `لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ` — which kind of `لَا`? *(`quran:2:2:1`)*
- **Answer:** **lā al-nāfiyah lil-jins** ('there is no'): `لَا` + indefinite **naṣb** noun with
  no tanwīn (`رَيْبَ`) → 'there is no doubt in it'. Carries a **two-vote / never-auto** gate
  ([`../evals/grammar-problems-matrix.md`](../evals/grammar-problems-matrix.md)).

**I12.** `مَا كَانَ مُحَمَّدٌ أَبَا أَحَدٍ مِّن رِّجَالِكُمْ` — is `مَا` negation here? *(`quran:33:40:1`)*
- **Procedure:** `مَا` + past verb in a declarative → negation; but `مَا` is multi-function.
- **Answer:** here **negation** → 'Muḥammad is not the father of any of your men'. Note
  `مُحَمَّدٌ` is the **name**, not the verb root ح-م-د ('to praise'). Default `مَا` to nothing
  elsewhere; `pending: multi_sense` when the frame is unclear.

## §4 — Relative vs. interrogative (مَا / مَنْ / أَيّ)

**I13.** `مَا هَٰذَا بَشَرًا` — is `مَا` interrogative or negating? *(`quran:12:31:11`)*
- **Answer:** **negating** `مَا` (Ḥijāzī, like لَيْسَ): 'this is not a human' — `بَشَرًا` is in
  naṣb as its khabar. Not the question 'what'. Frame: declarative + naṣb predicate.

**I14.** `مَا عِندَكُمْ يَنفَدُ` — relative or interrogative `مَا`? *(`quran:16:96:1`)*
- **Procedure:** `مَا` filling a **noun slot** before a clause → relative ('that which').
- **Answer:** **relative** `مَا` ('that which'): 'That which is with you will perish.' It is the
  mubtadaʾ; `يَنفَدُ` is the khabar.

**I15.** `مَنْ ذَا ٱلَّذِى يَشْفَعُ` — relative or interrogative `مَنْ`? *(`quran:2:255:18`)*
- **Procedure:** clause-initial, rhetorical question frame → interrogative.
- **Answer:** **interrogative** `مَنْ` ('who') → 'Who is it that can intercede…'. `is_man_who`
  → True (fatḥa-ish on the م, no shadda). Distinct from `مِنْ` 'from'.

**I16.** `فَبِأَىِّ ءَالَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ` — what is `أَىّ` doing? *(`quran:55:13:2`)*
- **Answer:** **interrogative** `أَىّ` ('which'), here in **jarr** after `بِـ` and itself muḍāf
  to `ءَالَآءِ` → 'so which of the favors of your Lord do you both deny?'. `أَىّ` can also be a
  relative ('whichever') or a vocative particle — **the frame decides**; `pending` if unclear.

## Intermediate drill rule

1. **Iḍāfa:** the head loses tanwīn/`ال`; **definiteness + relationship** come from the second
   term. Never gloss the head as indefinite when the construct is definite.
2. **Jār-majrūr:** the genitive noun is 'of …', governed by the preposition; a bi-/lām that
   carries a verb's transitivity folds into the verb.
3. **Negation:** the **governing negative** sets the tense — `لَمْ`→past, `لَنْ`→future, `لَا`+
   jussive→prohibition, `لَا`+indef-naṣb→'there is no', `مَا`→only sometimes negation.
4. **مَا/مَنْ/أَيّ:** noun-slot-before-clause → relative; question-initial → interrogative;
   declarative + naṣb predicate → negating `مَا`. Ambiguous → `pending: multi_sense`.
5. Ambiguous iḍāfa/jār-majrūr boundary and multi-function `مَا` → **two-vote**; **PENDING beats
   a wrong gloss.**
