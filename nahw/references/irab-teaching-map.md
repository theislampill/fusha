# Iʿrāb teaching map — zero → fluency for nominal & verbal sentences

The teaching spine for ajamī learners and the analysis spine for the engine. Each topic: the rule,
the signs, and the qamus-highlight relevance. Drill: [`../drills/irab-case-mood.md`](../drills/irab-case-mood.md).

## 1. The two sentence types
- **Jumla ismiyya** (nominal): mubtadaʾ (marfūʿ) + khabar (marfūʿ); "to be" is implied, not lexical.
- **Jumla fiʿliyya** (verbal): fiʿl + fāʿil (marfūʿ) + mafʿūl (manṣūb).

## 2. Iʿrāb of the noun (cases)
- **rafʿ** (ḍamma): mubtadaʾ, khabar, fāʿil, nāʾib al-fāʿil, ism kāna.
- **naṣb** (fatḥa): mafʿūl bih, ḥāl, tamyīz, khabar inna, ism … of the accusatives.
- **jarr** (kasra): after a preposition, or muḍāf ilayh.
- diptote (mamnūʿ min al-ṣarf): jarr sign is **fatḥa** (أَحْمَر, مَسَاجِد).

## 3. Iʿrāb of the verb (moods)
- **rafʿ** (ḍamma/nūn): muḍāriʿ unmarked.
- **naṣb** (fatḥa): after أَنْ/لَنْ/كَيْ/لام-taʿlīl.
- **jazm** (sukūn/ḥadhf): after لَمْ/لَمَّا/لام-amr/لا-nāhiya/conditional particles.
- **mabnī**: māḍī; amr; muḍāriʿ with نون-tawkīd or نون-niswa.

## 4. Construction relations
- **iḍāfa**: muḍāf (no tanwīn/no ال) + muḍāf ilayh (majrūr) → gloss the relationship + definiteness.
- **jar-majrūr / ẓarf**: gloss the phrase role, not just the lexeme (بِهِ "in it", إِلَيْنَا "to us" — not root ل-ي-ن).
- **naʿt/ṣifa**, **ʿaṭf**, **badal**, **tawkīd**: follower agrees with the followed in iʿrāb.

## 5. The accusatives (manṣūbāt) learners confuse
ḥāl (state, mushtaqq, "while X-ing") · tamyīz (specification, jāmid, "in respect of") · mafʿūl muṭlaq ·
mafʿūl li-ajlih · mafʿūl maʿah · munādā · mustathnā.

## 6. Sisters that flip iʿrāb
- **kāna & sisters**: rafʿ the ism, naṣb the khabar.
- **inna & sisters** (inna/anna/kaʾanna/lākinna/laʿalla/layta): naṣb the ism, rafʿ the khabar.
- **ẓanna & sisters** (أفعال القلوب): two manṣūb objects (the original mubtadaʾ/khabar).
- **lā of genus**: like inna; mufrad ism is **mabnī** on fatḥ.

## 7. Why this gates hover glosses
A gloss must respect the governing particle, case/mood, referent, and construction. A lexically
valid gloss is still wrong if the iʿrāb says otherwise (يَقْدِرُ "restricts" in a rizq contrast;
حَلِيم "forbearing" with a human referent; جِنَّة "madness"; أُمَّة "model"). Uncertain iʿrāb → pending,
and analysis+reasoning must BOTH be right (the GrammarProblems gate).
