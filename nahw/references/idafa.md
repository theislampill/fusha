# Iḍāfa (الإضافة) — the genitive construct

An iḍāfa is a possessive/genitive construct: a head noun (المضاف) immediately followed by a genitive noun
(المضاف إليه), with no article on the head and no tanwīn on the head. The pair forms one referential unit, and the
gloss should render the **relationship**, not two disconnected lexemes.

## Shape and signals

- The head noun loses its tanwīn and takes no ال: عَاصِمَةُ بَلَدِي "the capital of my country" (not "a capital").
- The second noun is **genitive** (majrūr): بَلَدِ‑ي, ٱللَّهِ, ٱلنَّاسِ.
- A pronoun can be the مضاف إليه: كِتَابُهُ "his book", رَبِّنَا "our Lord".

## Why it matters for the gloss

1. **Gloss the construct, not the bare head.** عِنْدَ ٱللَّهِ → "in the sight of Allah" (the construct head عِنْدَ
   takes its "in the sight of" sense from the divine مضاف إليه), not "near" in isolation. See
   [`rules/preposition-pronoun-rules.json`](../rules/preposition-pronoun-rules.json)#inda.
2. **Definiteness comes from the second term.** بَيْتُ ٱللَّهِ is definite ("the House of Allah") although the head
   bears no ال; do not gloss it as indefinite.
3. **A ẓarf (عِنْدَ, بَيْنَ, فَوْقَ, تَحْتَ) heads an iḍāfa of place/time** — render the spatial/temporal relation
   to the following noun, not the adverb alone.

## Common Qurʾānic constructs

عِبَادُ ٱللَّهِ "servants of Allah", يَوْمُ ٱلدِّينِ "the Day of Judgement", رَبُّ ٱلْعَالَمِينَ "Lord of the worlds",
أَهْلُ ٱلْكِتَابِ "the People of the Book", سَبِيلُ ٱللَّهِ "the path of Allah".

## Hand‑off

When a token is the head of an iḍāfa, its gloss must account for the construct (the relationship + the second
term's definiteness). If the second term is out of range / unknown, gloss the head's general sense and note the
construct, or pending — never assert a definiteness or relationship the construct doesn't license.
